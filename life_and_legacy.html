Life and Legacy

Wifredo Lam was a Cuban artist who lived from December 8, 1902 to September 11, 1982. He is regarded as one of the most important and influential Latin American artists of the 20th century. Lam was born in Sagua La Grande, Cuba, to a Chinese father and a mother of mixed African, European, and Indian descent. His mother was born to a Congolese slave mother and a Cuban father. In addition to a widely diverse heritage, Lam was also exposed to several religions through his family. These religions included Catholicism and practices associated with Yoruba and Afro-Cuban religions.


Wifredo Lam, Self-Portrait, 1926. Private collection, Paris © SDO Wifredo Lam

In 1916, at the age of 14, Lam moved to Havana to study painting and law. At this time, he also expanded his knowledge of Botany and Ecology. He then went to Europe to further his studies in art in 1923, where he became involved with the Surrealist movement and befriended Pablo Picasso and collaborated with artists such as André Breton, and Max Ernst. Through Picasso, Lam was introduced to Georges Braque, Joan Miró, Frida Kahlo and Diego Rivera. His time in Europe heavily influenced his artistic style, which incorporated elements of European Surrealism and Cubism.

Lam returned to Cuba in 1941, where he became involved with the country's artistic and intellectual circles. He was particularly interested in Afro-Cuban spirituality and syncretism, and his works often incorporated these themes. He also drew upon the traditions and folklore of Cuba's indigenous peoples. Around this time, Lam’s artistic style evolved to incorporate vegetation, abstract figures and animalistic features.

Wifredo Lam, atelier in Havana, 1947. Artwork © 2020 Artists Rights Society (ARS), New York / ADAGP, Paris

In 1946, Lam moved to France, where he continued to work on his art and became an important figure in the Parisian art scene. He exhibited his works in numerous solo and group exhibitions throughout his career, including the Venice Biennale, where he won the International Prize for Painting in 1952.

Wifredo Lam's legacy is multi-faceted and far-reaching. He is widely regarded as one of the most significant artists of the 20th century, and his influence can be seen in the work of many contemporary artists such as Roberto Matta (a Chilean artist who was closely associated with the Surrealist movement and known for his biomorphic and abstract works, which were influenced by Lam's style) and Wilfredo Arcay Ochandarena (a Cuban artist who was a student of Lam and adopted his style, incorporating Afro-Cuban themes and symbolism in his works).

Lam's incorporation of Afro-Cuban spirituality and his exploration of the African diaspora in his art was groundbreaking and helped to establish a new artistic vocabulary. His use of hidden and obscured figures, biomorphic forms, and fragmented shapes were also influential and have been widely imitated by other artists.

The Centro Wilfredo Lam is a contemporary art institution located in Havana, Cuba, and it is named after Wifredo Lam. The institute was founded in 1983 to honor Lam’s legacy and to promote contemporary art and cultural exchange in Cuba and beyond. The center is named after Lam and is dedicated to preserving his artistic legacy, as well as supporting and promoting contemporary artists who share his vision and values.

Lam's own artistic journey was heavily influenced by his Afro-Cuban roots, and his works often incorporated themes of spirituality, mythology, and cultural identity. The Centro Wilfredo Lam continues to promote these values through exhibitions, workshops, and other cultural events that celebrate and explore the rich artistic traditions of Cuba and the African diaspora.

In addition to promoting cultural exchange and artistic collaboration, the Centro Wilfredo Lam is also significant for its role in the preservation and documentation of Cuban art history. The center maintains an extensive archive of art and cultural materials related to Cuba and the African diaspora, and actively supports research and scholarship in these areas. The institution has organized significant exhibitions that have traveled globally, including the Havana Biennial.
